In the Post-Digital era, the borders between production and consumption are more than ever blended: the aura of content seems to have vanished. However, these projects reflect ways of reintroducing the notion of authenticity in the reapropriation in cultural production. Here you can find the full book, which covers topics such as What is Authenticity?; Post-Digital = Loss of Authenticity? and The Ressurgence of Authenticity.

ABOUT

This website was developed by Raquel Martins for the Masters in Design Communication, Faculty of Fine Arts of Lisbon, 1st semester 2022/23. It is complementary to a print edition, where all the research about production in the Post-Digital is gathered. In this book you will find three main chapters:

WHAT IS AUTHENTICITY?

The term “Aura” was introduced in the 19th century and popularized by Walter Benjamin to refer to the loss of singularity and authenticity of a work of art when it was reproduced mechanically. However, before his time, image production had other cultural and symbolic meanings that can help us establish bridges with Post-Digital production and questioning if the Aura resides only in the authenticity of a physical object or if the digital altered this notion and can present it to us in other forms.

POST-DIGITAL = LOST AUTHENTICITY?

Back to the present, the reflection about authenticity in cultural and artistic production is continued. In this chapter it is situated the Post-Digital and many of the problems around the creation of artifacts, not only for professionals but also to the world. A sort of crisis created by the (old) new ways of creation, where Aura as we knew it doesn’t seem to have place. This chapter raises questions and states the destruction of the status quo, as well as many of the traditions that we used to take for granted.

THE RESURGENCE OF AUTHENTICITY

A new definition is found for the concept of authenticity in the Post-Digital production and in its methods to work around the issues approached in the previous chapter. A call to action is made towards a more constructive future, giving examples of many who already do it. It is acknowledged that it’s difficult to fight many of the consequences and drive forces which led postproduction to a problematic momentum, but it is possible to take as a challenge the creation of new narratives from pre-existing ones with new protocols of use. New ways of seeing, creating and raising new questions. Being copies or not, it is the articulation between meaning and production that allows us to reencounter Aura in creation.
